## Lesson Contexts

### Kindergarten — Squishy Circuits

Students built a fan circuit together as a class team using Play-Doh, batteries, cables, and a switch. One student per table rotated through contributor roles (holding conductors, connecting cables, controlling the switch) while the class watched, named parts, and counted down together. In the follow-up session, students built squishy circuit airplanes in small groups, applying vocabulary and design knowledge from the demo.

**Evidence focus:** vocabulary development, collective participation, design literacy, aesthetic fulfillment, schema-building.

### Grade 4 — Minecraft Education Agent Challenge

Students solved a timed dual-plate activation puzzle by coding an agent to reach one gold plate while the student character reached the other, within twenty seconds. Students who finished early were redirected into peer-teaching roles. Piter circulated, counted blocks out loud with struggling students, and helped students read and narrate environment-based problems.

**Evidence focus:** debugging, persistence, spatial reasoning, peer teaching, mathematical literacy, inquiry.
