## Context

A kindergarten session with two teaching phases. In the first phase, Piter and co-teacher Rami ran a whole-class Squishy Circuits fan and light demonstration using a contributor model — one student per table rotated through the demo roles while the class watched and responded. In the second phase, Piter and Rami previewed a video on how to build a squishy circuit airplane and named the parts together with students, setting up the small-group airplane build for the next class.

## Moment 2 — Circuit Demo: Vocabulary and Collective Participation

*Supports: Example 5 — Joy by Design: Collective Circuit Demo*

Students are gathered at the front. Piter introduces the circuit components and begins the vocabulary-building exchange.

**Piter:** Do any of you know what this is? Raise your hand. Battery. So — is it battery, right? What else?

**Student:** It's like wires.

**Piter:** So it's a battery with wires, that's true. What else? Anything else?

**Student:** Like, um, it's like you can use it for remotes sometimes.

**Piter:** You can use that as a remote. What do you do with a remote? Turn on and off, right? So what do you call something that turns on and off?

**Student:** A switch.

**Piter:** A switch. There you go. It's a switch. It's a switch with batteries and cables, right?

*Students connect the circuit components one at a time — one holds the Play-Doh conductors, one connects the cables, one controls the switch:*

**Piter:** So where do we connect the red one? First we need the insulator — we need that bowl, right? Can you plug the bowl in there? Yeah, can you hold it? Yeah, if you can plug it in — there you go.

**Piter:** Now — who's gonna turn on the switch? You guys wanna count to three and turn it on? Three!

**Together:** Three! Two! One!

**Piter:** There you go! So let's show this fan, everyone. You guys see this?

## Moment 3 — Introducing the Airplane Build

*Supports: Example 6 — Joy as Making: Squishy Circuits Airplane Build*

After the fan demo, Piter introduces the airplane-building challenge and previews the video with students.

**Piter:** So next class, we're gonna have a little competition. You guys are gonna build an airplane. You're gonna make sure that the airplane can fly by making sure that it turns on. The winner — the one that does it, that does a good job, a good and a clean job — is gonna have a reward. I have some prizes in here for you guys.

**Piter:** You guys wanna see how the airplane is built? Alright. We're gonna show you a video on how to build the airplane. And then when we come next class, we're just gonna jump to it.

*During the video — naming parts together with students:*

**Piter:** You see that tool over there, right? You guys are gonna be using this to flatten the Play-Doh. What do you think this is?

**Students:** Play-Doh!

**Piter:** This is the body of the airplane. Remember to use this to cut. You see, with this you can cut the clay. And this is the body of the airplane.

**Rami:** That over there is a wing. So when you fly the blue one, you put that on top, and then you fold it — and that's gonna be your wing. And then you do the same thing for the small one. And that's the wheel.

**Piter:** And the winner is gonna get one of those amazing tickets. You guys looking forward to that?
